# Knowledge Distillation

Knowledge distillation trains a student model to match a teacher model. The teacher can be a larger model, an ensemble, a stronger checkpoint, or a model with access to a richer training signal.

Given teacher logits $z^{(T)}$ and student logits $z^{(S)}$, temperature-scaled probabilities are:

$$
p_T(k\mid x)
=
\operatorname{softmax}\left(\frac{z^{(T)}_k}{\tau}\right),
\qquad
p_S(k\mid x)
=
\operatorname{softmax}\left(\frac{z^{(S)}_k}{\tau}\right)
$$

where $\tau>0$ is the distillation temperature. Higher $\tau$ softens the teacher distribution and exposes similarity between classes.

The distillation loss is often a KL divergence or cross-entropy between teacher and student probabilities:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{KD}}
=
\tau^2
\operatorname{KL}
\left(
p_T(\cdot\mid x)
\;\|\;
p_S(\cdot\mid x)
\right)
$$

The $\tau^2$ factor is commonly used to keep gradient scale comparable across temperatures.

When hard labels $y$ are available, training can combine supervised and distillation losses:

$$
\mathcal{L}
=
\alpha\,
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{sup}}(p_S,y)
+
(1-\alpha)\,
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{KD}}
$$

where $\alpha\in[0,1]$ controls the hard-label versus teacher-signal tradeoff.

## Uses

- Compress a large model into a smaller deployable model.
- Transfer behavior from an ensemble or stronger checkpoint.
- Train a specialized model from weak labels generated by a teacher.
- Distill generation, ranking, retrieval, or policy behavior into a cheaper model.

## Risks

- The student can inherit teacher errors and biases.
- Teacher confidence can hide uncertainty if probabilities are poorly calibrated.
- Distillation may improve average performance while hurting rare cases.
- Synthetic teacher labels can leak benchmark or private information if the data source is not controlled.

## Checks

- What is the teacher, and what data did it see?
- Are teacher outputs logits, probabilities, labels, rationales, trajectories, or preferences?
- Is the student evaluated against ground truth, not only teacher agreement?
- Are temperature, loss weights, and teacher checkpoint documented?
- Does distillation preserve calibration and out-of-distribution behavior?
- Are private teacher prompts, hidden datasets, or unpublished results omitted from public notes?
